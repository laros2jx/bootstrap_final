Explore Our Favorite Destinations:


Iceland

Reykjavik
  • The vibrant capital city with colorful buildings, lively cafes, and unique arts scene. A great base for exploring nearby geothermal spas and scenic spots.
Akureyri
  • Known as the “Capital of the North,” this charming town offers whale-watching tours, beautiful botanical gardens, and proximity to the stunning Lake Mývatn.
Vik
  • A small coastal village famous for its black sand beaches, dramatic cliffs, and views of the Reynisdrangar sea stacks.


Italy

Rome
  • A city where ancient history comes alive, from the Colosseum to the Vatican. Explore vibrant neighborhoods and enjoy classic Italian cuisine.
Florence
  • The heart of Renaissance art and architecture, home to Michelangelo’s David, the Duomo, and an array of world-renowned art galleries.
Venice
  • A unique city of canals, bridges, and gondolas, with stunning architecture like St. Mark's Basilica and the enchanting charm of Piazza San Marco.


New Zealand

Queenstown
  • The adventure capital of New Zealand, ideal for skiing, bungee jumping, and breathtaking hikes along Lake Wakatipu and the Southern Alps.
Auckland
  • A bustling city known for its beautiful harbors, diverse dining scene, and easy access to nearby islands and beaches.
Wellington
  • The country's capital, known for its creative culture, museums like Te Papa, and beautiful waterfront area with vibrant cafes and shops.


Morocco

Marrakech
  • Known for its lively souks, historic palaces, and the bustling Djemaa el-Fna square filled with music, performers, and food stalls.
Chefchaouen
  • Often called the “Blue Pearl,” this city in the Rif Mountains is famous for its picturesque blue-washed streets and relaxed atmosphere.
Fes
  • A historic city with one of the world’s oldest universities, a sprawling medina, and traditional crafts, Fes is a cultural gem.


Canada

Toronto
  • Canada’s largest city, offering a mix of cultures, neighborhoods, iconic sites like the CN Tower, and a thriving arts and food scene.
Vancouver
  • Surrounded by mountains and sea, this city is known for its scenic beauty, outdoor activities, and vibrant neighborhoods like Gastown and Yaletown.
Montreal
  • A city with a European flair, known for its festivals, historic Old Montreal, and a blend of French and English cultures.
